Dominos

Domino's Pizza, Inc., commonly referred to as Domino's, is an American multinational pizza restaurant chain founded in 1960 and led by CEO Russell Weiner. The corporation is Delaware-domiciled and headquartered at the Domino's Farms Office Park in Ann Arbor Township, near Ann Arbor, Michigan.As of 2018, Domino's had approximately 15,000 stores, with 5,649 in the United States, 1,500 in India, and 1,249 in the United Kingdom.Domino's has stores in over 83 countries and 5,701 cities worldwide.

History

Domino's Pizza was founded in 1960 by Tom and James Monaghan in Ypsilanti, Michigan, originally called "DomiNick's." After Tom bought out his brother, the company rebranded as Domino's and focused on quick pizza delivery. In 1967, Domino's opened its first franchise, sparking rapid expansion across the U.S. and internationally. Known for its "30 minutes or less" delivery guarantee (later discontinued for safety), Domino's revolutionized pizza delivery. Today, the company remains a global leader, constantly innovating with technology and new offerings to enhance the customer experience.
